The big picture

Delivery plan

Three phases, each with an output you can check

Scoring foundation first, the surface it lives on second, then calibration against your own known cases. Every phase ends on a verifiable result, not a status update.

Phase 1
Data and scoring foundation
Connect to the customer intelligence database, pull the highest-weight and direct-source signals, and build the engine: composite rating, typed tags, segment-aware weighting, renewal-proximity multiplier. Output: a working risk score per account for Core and Premier, verifiable in data.
Phase 2
The dashboard surface
The ranked view on the internal dashboard - rating, typed tags, semantic summaries, evidence links, and the owner and segment filters, plus the calibration controls where Jack tunes weights and thresholds. Daily refresh in place. Output: the dashboard Success and Support actually use.
Phase 3
Calibration, consolidation, and QA
Validate the model against Jack's known churned and at-risk accounts, tune until known cases surface, fold in the existing artifacts, and run full QA. Output: a calibrated, trusted, single-source-of-truth dashboard.
How I work: the shape is set up front; the details firm up organically as real accounts move through the model. Some signals will prove stronger than expected, others noisier - which is exactly why the scoring is tunable and calibration is a real step, not an afterthought.

Why it's trustworthy
Before it's called done, the model runs against real churned and at-risk accounts you already know. If it misses accounts everyone knew were in trouble, it isn't working. That's the pass/fail test.

Evidence, not opinion

These systems are non-deterministic, so "does the risk model work" can't rest on a claim. Running it against accounts Insightful already knows went wrong turns the question into a matter of evidence - the known cases either surface as risky, or the model isn't finished.

Explainability is what makes it acted on

Every flag carries a typed breakdown and a plain-English reason, so Success and Support can tell a real problem from a known-and-handled one, and prioritize between multiple critical accounts. A team that can see why an account is flagged will work the list.

The definition of risk is expected to sharpen as the model runs - which is why the scoring is tunable and calibration is a real, bounded step rather than a one-time guess on day one.
